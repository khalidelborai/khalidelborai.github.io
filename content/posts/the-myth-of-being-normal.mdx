# Social normality

In social contexts normality refers to what is considered *typical* or expected behaviour. 

It is a social construct that is used to define what is acceptable and what is not.

This can include things like social norms, gender roles, or expectations around certain behaviors 
(e.g. getting married, having children, pursuing a certain career path).


On one hand, social norms and expectations can be helpful in how we navigate our social interactions
and relationships with others.

They can help us understand what is expected of us in certain situations, and they can also provide
a sense of belonging and community when we share similar values or beliefs with others.

On the other hand, social norms and expectations can also be harmful in how they limit our freedom
to express ourselves and live our lives as we see fit.

## Societal expectations

Societal expectations are the unwritten rules and norms that dictate how individuals are expected to behave within a given culture or community.

They are often deeply ingrained, and may be so taken for granted that they are not even consciously recognized as expectations.

### Societal expectations effect on individuals

1. #### Identity formation

        Societal expectations can influence how we see ourselves, and how we form our identities.
        
        For example neurodivergent people may feel like they don't fit in with society's expectations of what it means to be "normal".

2. #### Social interactions

        Societal expectations can influence how we interact with others, shaping our communication style, social skills, and emotional regulation.
        
        For example, social norms around politeness and respect may influence how we express our opinions or deal with conflict in social situations.
