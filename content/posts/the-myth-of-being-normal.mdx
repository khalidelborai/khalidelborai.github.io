# Social normality

In social contexts, normality refers to what is considered *typical* or expected behavior. 

It is a social construct that defines what is acceptable and what is not.

This can include social norms, or expectations around certain behaviors. 


On the one hand, social norms and expectations can be helpful in how we navigate our social interactions
and relationships with others.

They can help us understand what is expected of us in certain situations, and they can also provide
a sense of belonging and community when we share similar values or beliefs.

On the other hand, social norms and expectations can also be harmful in how they limit our freedom
to express ourselves and live our lives as we see fit.

## Societal expectations

Societal expectations are unwritten rules and norms that dictate how individuals are expected to behave within a given culture or community.

They are often deeply ingrained and may be so taken for granted that they are not consciously recognized as expectations.

### How societal expectations affect individuals

1. #### Identity formation

        Societal expectations can influence how we see ourselves and form our identities.
        
        Identity formation is a complex process involving various cultural, social, and personal experiences. 

        One of the most significant factors influencing identity formation is societal expectations. 
        Societal expectations refer to the norms, values, beliefs, and standards a society expects its members to adhere to. 
        
        The impact of societal expectations on identity formation can also be seen in neurodivergent individuals. 
        Society often expects individuals to conform to specific standards of behavior and communication, which can be difficult for those with neurodivergent conditions such as autism or ADHD.
        These expectations can lead to feelings of isolation and exclusion and difficulties in social interactions and employment opportunities.
  
        Society needs to recognize and accommodate neurodivergent individuals, allowing them to thrive in their unique way without fear of judgment or discrimination.

        In conclusion, societal expectations play a significant role in identity formation. These expectations can impact an individual's sense of self-worth, their behavior, and their relationships with others. While some societal expectations can be positive, it is important to recognize that they can also be limiting and prevent individuals from fully expressing themselves. It is essential to create a society that is inclusive and accepting of diversity in all its forms to support healthy identity formation.

2. #### Social interactions

        Societal expectations can influence how we interact with others, shaping our communication style, social skills, and emotional regulation.
        
        For example, social norms around politeness and respect may influence how we express our opinions or deal with conflict in social situations.
